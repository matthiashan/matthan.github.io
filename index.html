What I Do

I'm a massive data nerd for lack of a better term! My journey began as a research associate at St. Michael's Hospital working on a longitudinal study about how the Housing First Model affected the quality of life and health care of homeless individuals suffering from mental health issues.

Searching for a new opportunity, I pivoted into marketing and worked as a content specialist in the tech space by designing in-app interactive ads but my heart and passion was set on a career in data.

I took the plunge, put all my eggs in one basket and began my journey into data science in 2020. Although I'm new to this field, I love working on projects and developing insights that will not only help drive business intelligence but also create an impact in our ever-evolving world. I specialize in a variety of software and code ranging from SQL, Tableau, Python, Microsoft Office, macOS and Git.

Who I Am

Proudly born and raised in Toronto, Canada to parents of Mauritian descent, I grew up in Scarborough and attended French immersion elementary school before transitioning to Brebeuf College School in my formative years. I completed my post-secondary education at the University of Toronto, George Brown College and Brainstation to further my professional career.

My passion has always been sports. I remember having a deep affinity for professional sports as a child, watching hours of games and highlights past my bedtime much to the chagrin of my parents. I quickly gravitated towards the Leafs, Raptors, Jays, Patriots and Liverpool Football Club (YNWA!) as my teams of choice. When my hopes of becoming a professional athlete were dashed, sports analytics was taking off and I found a new field of interest. As data became an essential part of our every day lives, my interest expanded into its versatile use with the potential to make it a life-long career.

Personal growth drives my zest for life! I am always trying to improve my mental and physical health whether it's through my active lifestyle like playing in a variety of activites like Muay Thai, spikeball, softball, hiking, portaging and hockey in the winter, or learning from incredible people like Brene Brown, David Goggins, Jen Sincero, Tim Bilyeu, and Mark Manson to name a few.

I'm am nomadic by nature and backpacking has a huge influence on my life. I've travelled months at a time with nothing but a backpack to almost 20 countries around the world, learning about culture, architecture, history and most importantly people. I believe that it gives a deeper understanding of our world, a connection to the people living in it and gratitude for our own experiences.
